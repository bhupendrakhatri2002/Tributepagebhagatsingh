ABOUT BHAGAT SINGH


1. Bhagat Singh was born on September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, which is now in Pakistan.

2. He belonged to a Sikh family deeply rooted in patriotism and freedom struggle.

3. Bhagat Singh's family had a history of involvement in political and social movements against British rule.

4. He was deeply influenced by the sacrifices of his uncle, Ajit Singh, and the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre in 1919.

5. Bhagat Singh joined the Non-Cooperation Movement led by Mahatma Gandhi at a young age.

6. He later became disillusioned with non-violence as a sole means of achieving independence and turned to more radical methods.

7. Bhagat Singh, along with his associates, founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha to promote revolutionary ideas among the youth.

8. He was a member of the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA), a revolutionary organization that advocated armed resistance against the British.

9. Bhagat Singh was involved in various acts of protest against British rule, including the famous assembly bombing in 1929.

10. During the assembly bombing, he and Batukeshwar Dutt threw non-lethal smoke bombs to protest repressive laws.

11. Bhagat Singh and his associates went on a hunger strike in prison to demand better treatment for political prisoners.

12. The hunger strike, which lasted 116 days, garnered widespread support and attention.

13. He was a prolific writer and used his writings to explain his revolutionary ideas and vision for a free India.

14. Bhagat Singh believed in socialism and the need for social and economic equality in an independent India.

15. He was deeply influenced by the works of revolutionaries like Karl Marx, Vladimir Lenin, and others.

16. Bhagat Singh's most famous slogan was "Inquilab Zindabad," meaning "Long Live the Revolution."

17. He was arrested for his involvement in the killing of British police officer J.P. Saunders and later faced trial.

18. Despite widespread appeals for clemency, Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, was hanged on March 23, 1931.

19. His execution sparked outrage and protest across India, and he became a symbol of resistance against colonialism.

20. Bhagat Singh's legacy continues to inspire people in their fight for justice and freedom, and he is remembered annually on Shaheed Diwas (Martyrs' Day) on March 23.

21. Several movies, books, and documentaries have been made to commemorate his life and sacrifices.

22. Bhagat Singh's vision and dedication to the cause of independence remain an integral part of India's history and the struggle for freedom.
